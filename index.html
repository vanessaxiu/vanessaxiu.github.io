Free STBBI* Risk Assessment Service
*STBBI: Sexually Transmitted and Blood-Borne Infection

In the past 20 years, there has been a steady increase in rate of a number of sexually transmitted and blood-borne infections (STBBIs).
From 2014 to 2018, there was a record-high increase in syphilis (151%), gonorrhoea (110%), and chlamydia (18%).
Stigma and discrimination against marginalized groups complicates existing barriers and increases inaccessibility to healthcare and prevention. The good news is, you can take a free test to assess your risk for STBBI.
Our test is evidence-guided and reflects your unique risk profile.
